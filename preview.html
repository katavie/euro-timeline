476

Fall of Rome

The gradual decline of the Roman Empire was marked with political instability as emperors were assassinated quickly after taking the throne. Reforms by Emperors Diocletian and Constantine failed to solve the socioeconomic problems threatening the empire. In 476, Odoacer, a Germanic leader, expelled the Roman emperor, signalling the formal “fall” of the Rome to foreign invaders.

1848

Muhammad's Hijra

Muhammad, an Arabian merchant disturbed by the immorality of Meccan society, heard an angel calling him to be the messenger of God. His teachings were harshly rejected, so he and his followers journeyed from Mecca to Yathrib, later renamed Medina (city of the Prophet), in a trip known as the hijra.

800

Charlemagne Crowned Emperor

Charlemagne, grandson of Charles Martel, who had victoriously led Christians against invading Muslims, became King of the Franks in 768. During his 46-year reign, Charlemagne united Western Europe and was crowned Emperor of the Romans on Christmas Day for assisting Pope Leo III suppress rebels. Charlemagne sought to further improve his realm through Christianity and education.
